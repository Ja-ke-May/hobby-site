UK Freshwater Fish Species

*For an optimal view, please rotate your device to landscape mode

Pike (Esox lucius) are a predatory fish in UK freshwater known for its voracious appetite, sharp teeth, and impressive sizes. Anglers use techniques like lure fishing for this olive-green species, providing a thrilling and challenging fishing experience.

Common carp (Cyprinus carpio) are a well-known freshwater inhabitant in the UK. Anglers employ techniques like bottom fishing and specific baits for this captivating bronze to golden species with unique scales and barbels.

Mirror carp (Cyprinus carpio mirror), a unique variant in UK freshwater, they feature irregular, reflective scales. Anglers prize these fish due to their elusive nature and striking appearance.

Other carp species, including golden-scaled crucians, hybrids, wild carp, and the unexpected presence of koi, create a varied and enticing challenge for anglers in UK fishing lakes.

Perch (Perca fluviatilis), a predatory fish, they are sought after for their hunting skills and distinctive stripe pattern with a spiny dorsal fin.

Tench (Tinca tinca), a distinct species in the UK, they offer anglers a unique and rewarding catch. With a dark greenish-brown hue, leathery feel, and elusive nature, the tench stands out among British fish.

Bream (Abramis brama), are known for their silvery scales and deep-bodied shape. Anglers target bream using methods like bottom fishing, capitalizing on their social nature.

Barbel (Barbus barbus), prized in UK freshwater, are known for their muscular build and spirited fight. Anglers use ledgering and float fishing to entice these powerful fish.

Chub (Squalius cephalus), popular in UK freshwater, they attract anglers with a robust build and distinctive appearance.
